Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

We are annotating binary cognitive concern over the calendar years 2017-2018. We aim to answer two questions:

1. Is there any documented suspicion/concern of memory or cognitive decline?

Options:
  • No Cognitive Concern
  • Cognitive Concern
  • Undetermined

Cognitive concern could be relayed from (1) patients (e.g., "patient complains of memory problems"), (2) family members and friends (e.g., "sister says she is worried about the patientâ€™s memory"), or 3. providers in the notes, phone logs, indications for imaging procedures or other tests, etc.

To annotate as "no cognitive concern," there should at least be implicit evidence of no cognitive concerns (e.g., they are still working, clearly managing their own care or hobbies, can follow complicated instructions, or have annual wellness or specialist notes with multisystem assessment and no mention of a cognitive concern). If a cognition test was performed with an explicit note of intact cognition, then even better. Alert and oriented alone is not sufficient.

Choose undetermined if sufficient information in the EHR is not available.

2. What is the stage of cognitive impairment?

Options:
  • No Cognitive Impairment
  • Cognitive Impairment Not Dementia (CIND)
    • Subjective Cognitive Concern (SCC)
    • Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI)
    • Undetermined
  • Dementia
    • Mild
    • Moderate
    • Severe
    • Undetermined

If cognitive concerns are present, then label the stage of impairment as either CIND (Cognitive Impairment Not Dementia) or Dementia.

The stage of dementia is determined by activities of daily living (i.e. IADLs/ADLs). Explicit documentation of dementia by a provider in the EHR is not necessary (e.g., impairment in one or more cognitive/functional impairments that interferes with independence might be independently documented by different providers who remain unaware of the "big picture" or "progression").

If there is no evidence of dementia, choose CIND. If there is at least minimal normal cognitive testing, choose SCC, otherwise choose MCI. If there is no cognitive testing, choose undetermined.
